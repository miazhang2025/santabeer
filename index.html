The Idea

A Joke Hidden Inside an Ad
Hidden Inside a Joke.

Weight-loss brands hire two different people to fake "before and after." We made that the punchline.

The concept came from a simple cultural observation: pharmaceutical and wellness ads routinely cast two actors to manufacture transformation — a quiet deception dressed up as inspiration.

We pushed that convention to its most absurd extreme — Santa Claus, of all people, getting an Ozempic glow-up — then pulled the curtain back. The real punchline isn't the slim Santa. It's two exhausted actors sharing a cold beer in a parking lot after the shoot. Good products don't need performance. Genuine happiness doesn't either.

How We Made It

The Pipeline

01
TapNow
Storyboard & Shot Planning

Before generating a single frame, we planned the full shot list in TapNow — compositions, camera logic, timing of the meta-reveal. Locking the narrative structure early prevented costly generation detours later.

02
ComfyUI Nano Banana
Style Lock & Character Design

We established a clay stop-motion aesthetic — warm, handmade, slightly uncanny — as a deliberate counterpoint to the ad's cold irony. Locking character references in ComfyUI and Nano Banana before video generation was essential for consistency across every shot.

03
Claude Gemini
Script & Prompt Engineering

Claude and Gemini were used to refine shot descriptions, sharpen prompt language for each scene, and iterate on the final punchline until it landed without over-explaining. The tagline — Real Taste, No Acting Required — went through multiple passes before it felt inevitable.

04
Nano Banana Kling Runway Gen-4
Keyframe-First Video Generation

Rather than prompting video cold, we generated key frames in Nano Banana first, then iterated to produce refined intermediate frames. These served as anchors for Kling and Runway — which generated full motion sequences between them. This approach gave us significantly more control over character continuity and motion than starting from text alone.

05
ElevenLabs Premiere Pro
Audio & Final Edit

A rough cut was assembled first, then ElevenLabs generated character voices and key ambient audio against that edit. Final integration and polish happened in Premiere Pro — with the rough cut informing every audio decision rather than working the other way around.

The Hard Part

When Santa Gets Stuck,
So Does the Prompt.

The Chimney Shot

The cross-section shot of Santa wedged in the chimney — the visual anchor of the entire ad's premise — refused to generate cleanly. Every prompt combination produced something wrong: wrong angle, wrong proportions, missing the black comedy of the situation.

✕

Attempted direct text-to-video prompting across multiple iterations — no prompt produced a usable cross-section view.

→

Built a precise reference illustration in Photoshop — a hybrid human-AI workflow, drawing the exact geometry and proportions manually.

✓

Used the Photoshop illustration as a generation anchor. The result became one of the most visually distinctive frames in the piece.
